What a $200 campaign actually buys

Say you run a casual spot and set an $8 bounty. You top up your campaign wallet with $200. That's up to 25 verified customer visits — each one a real person who ordered food at your restaurant, confirmed by your own staff. If those guests average a $40 check, your wallet drove roughly $1,000 in orders at a fixed, known cost per customer.

Compare that with click-based advertising, where the same $200 buys taps on a screen and you never learn how many became customers.

Your spend is capped by design

  • Campaigns are prepaid — the wallet is the budget, and it can't go negative.
  • A campaign can't activate unless the wallet covers at least one bounty.
  • Campaigns pause automatically when the balance drops below the bounty.
  • Every wallet movement is backed by a double-entry ledger you can audit in the app.
