How it works

From claiming your page to your first verified customer.

Flavorites Business is designed so a busy owner can set everything up from a phone, and a busy server can verify a visit in seconds. Here's the whole journey.

  1. Claim your restaurant page

    Search for your restaurant in the Flavorites Business app and claim it by verifying your business email. If your restaurant isn't on Flavorites yet, create the page — new pages are reviewed by the Flavorites team and typically go live within 24–48 hours. Claim approval is what unlocks publishing rights for your page. More on claiming.

  2. Publish your storefront

    Your profile is prefilled from the listing data Flavorites already has — name, address, hours, cuisine, photos — so you're editing, not retyping. Add your story, menu highlights, and your own photos. Everything you publish updates the same restaurant page customers see when they search and browse the Flavorites app.

  3. Invite your team

    Add employees with a focused role: they can verify QR visit passes and enter order amounts, but can't touch campaign settings, wallets, payouts, or your storefront. Owners keep full control.

  4. Create and fund a referral campaign

    Set a fixed bounty you'll pay per verified customer — most restaurants choose between $4 and $25 depending on their average check. Fund a prepaid campaign wallet (minimum $100 top-up) with a card via secure Stripe checkout. Your campaign can't activate unless the wallet covers at least one bounty, and it pauses automatically when the balance runs low.

  5. Choose your rules

    By default, any customer referred by a fan qualifies. Prefer to pay only for growth? Enable first-time-only mode so only a customer's first verified visit earns a reward. You can also set a minimum order subtotal, require referrers to have a minimum follower count, or run a private campaign limited to fans you invite.

  6. Fans share, customers arrive with a visit pass

    Referrers must have your restaurant saved as one of their Flavorites — these are genuine fans, not ad brokers. When they recommend you, the customer gets a QR visit pass that's valid for 7 days, one-time use, and tied to your restaurant.

  7. Your staff scans and verifies

    At the table or the counter, your team scans the customer's QR pass in the app and enters the order subtotal (and optionally the items ordered, if you've set up menu highlights). The bounty is reserved from your campaign wallet at that moment — you always know exactly what you've spent.

  8. The dispute window protects you

    Every reward stays pending for 72 hours. If something looks off, dispute it from the app and the Flavorites team reviews it before any money moves to the referrer.

  9. Flavorites handles the payouts

    After the dispute window, rewards become available to referrers and are paid out in weekly batches through Stripe Connect. You never handle referrer payments, tax forms, or cash.

  10. Watch the results

    Your reporting dashboard shows redeemed referrals, new customers, referral revenue from entered subtotals, and which referrers bring you the most business — so you can tune your bounty and rules with real numbers.
